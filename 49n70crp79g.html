The above R program generates the boxplot shown in Fig. \ref{935989}. There is a difference in gene expression values of the Tc4 in ALL versus AML patients as shown in Fig. \ref{935989}. In order to test the null hypothesis, we can apply a Welch two-sample t-test to determine whether the means of two gene expressions of populations are statistically different. The null hypothesis is:
    H0 : µ1 = µ2
where
  • μ1 is the mean expression for gene tc4 in ALL patients
  • μ2 is the mean expression for gene tc4 in AML patients
Let's suppose that \(\overline{x}\) is the mean of the gene expression data for the group ALL and \(\overline{y}\) is the mean of the population AML, and \(s_2^1\) the variance of the first and \(s_2^2\) that of the second, for {x1, · · ·,xn} and {y1, · · · ,ym} the data for ALL and AML respectively. Then the t-statistic can be formulated as:
\(\frac{(\overline{x}−\overline{y})−(\mu_1−\mu_2)}{\sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n}+\frac{s_2^2}{m}}}\)
The decision procedure with respect to the null-hypothesis is now completely similar to the above t-test. Note that the t-value is large if the difference between x and y is large and the standard deviations s1 and s2 are small.
